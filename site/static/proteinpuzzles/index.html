A 3D Jigsaw for Science

 

Overview

Your mission, should you choose to accept it, is to solve the puzzle of the structure of the protein complex using the information provided. Each puzzle includes individual proteins (tiny parts of cells and the puzzle pieces) and different experimental data that serve as guard rails to form the correct arrangement of the pieces. The more experimental data you satisfy, the higher score, and the closer you are to solving the puzzle and becoming a scientist!

How to Play



Download & Test

Protein Puzzles public beta is now available to play as a free mobile app for iOS and Android! Follow the instructions to download and install the game. Please provide game feedback here.

The current release includes a set of training puzzles intended to demonstrate gameplay. We are building support for additional puzzles in which the optimal structure is unknown, allowing citizen scientists to contribute to solving real protein complex structures. These player solutions will then be experimentally validated by researchers. Help us achieve that vision by playtesting and providing feedback!

 

Teaching

Protein puzzles are based on experimental restraints derived from real biochemical and bioinformatic techniques. This game serves as an excellent educational tool for those who are interested in learning or teaching about biophysical techniques, functions of complexes, and how structure and function are coupled. Keep an eye out for new puzzles that might be relevant to your biology course.

 

Protein Puzzles was created in the Taylor Lab at the University of Texas, Austin in partnership with Vital Mind Media.
